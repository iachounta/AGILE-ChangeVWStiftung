Using generative AI for lesson planning

Instruction sheet #03

A step-by-step guide for teachers: how to use AI tools to support — not replace — your lesson planning process.

School level
K-12 (all levels)
Time needed
15 – 30 min
AI literacy
Beginner
1
Before you start ▼
Define your learning objective first. Write down what students should know or be able to do by the end of the lesson — before opening any AI tool. The AI supports your plan; it does not replace your pedagogical judgement.
Know your context. Have ready: subject, grade level, lesson duration, any curricular constraints, and the prior knowledge of your students. The more context you give the AI, the more relevant its suggestions will be.
Choose your tool. Common options include ChatGPT, Microsoft Copilot, or Gemini. Check your school's approved tool list first. Do not enter student names or personal data into any AI tool.
Tip

Use a free account for getting started. You do not need a paid subscription for basic lesson planning support.

2
Writing your prompt ▼
Structure your prompt in three parts: (1) role — tell the AI it is helping a teacher; (2) task — what you want it to produce; (3) context — subject, grade, duration, and learning objective.
Example prompt: "You are helping a secondary school science teacher. Create a 45-minute lesson plan on photosynthesis for Year 8 students (age 12–13). The learning objective is: students can explain the inputs and outputs of photosynthesis. Include a starter activity, main activity, and exit ticket."
Iterate. If the first output is not quite right, refine: "Make the starter activity more hands-on" or "Adapt this for students with lower reading levels."
Tip

Save prompts that work well. Over time you will build a personal library of effective prompts tailored to your subjects and teaching style.

3
Reviewing and adapting the output ▼
Always review before using. AI-generated lesson plans may contain factual errors, culturally inappropriate content, or activities unsuitable for your students. You are responsible for what happens in your classroom.
Check alignment with your curriculum. Does the activity match your national or school curriculum requirements? Does the level match your students' actual ability?
Add your expertise. Insert your own examples, local references, and knowledge of your specific students. The AI has no knowledge of your class — you do.
Important

Do not copy AI output directly into official school documents or submit it as your own professional work without review and significant adaptation.

4
Checklist before your lesson ▼
0 of 6 completed
  • I defined my learning objective before using the AI tool
  • I checked that no student personal data was entered into the AI tool
  • I reviewed all AI-generated content for factual accuracy
  • I verified that activities align with my curriculum requirements
  • I adapted the plan to reflect my knowledge of my students
  • I could deliver this lesson without the AI output if needed
