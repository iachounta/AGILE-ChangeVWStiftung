How to choose an appropriate AI tool for your classroom

Instruction sheet #01

A decision guide for K-12 teachers: match your learning objective, school level, subject, and applicable regulations to the right AI tool — before you start.

1
Decision helper — find your tool match ▼

Answer the four questions below to receive a tailored recommendation for your context.

2
What to look for in any AI tool ▼
Age appropriateness. Check the tool's minimum age in its terms of service. Most general-purpose AI tools require users to be 13 or 18 depending on the country. For primary school, look for education-specific platforms designed for younger learners.
Data privacy and GDPR compliance. Any tool used with students must comply with GDPR or equivalent national law. Avoid tools requiring students to create personal accounts unless your school has a Data Processing Agreement in place. Never enter student names, photos, or identifying information.
Content moderation. Does the tool have safeguards against inappropriate content? Education-focused tools typically include classroom-grade filters. Test general-purpose tools carefully before use with students.
Transparency and explainability. Can students and you understand how the tool works at a basic level? Prefer tools that do not present AI-generated content as factual without caveats.
Cost and access equity. Will all students be able to access the tool equally? Consider free vs. paid tiers, device requirements, and accessibility for students with disabilities.
Tip

Before introducing any tool to your class, test it yourself for at least 30 minutes. Try to produce content that could be inappropriate — if safeguards fail in testing, do not use it with students.

3
Relevant regulations and frameworks ▼

These frameworks apply across European school systems and should inform your tool choice.

EU AI Act (2024)
AI systems used in education are classified as high-risk. Providers must ensure transparency, human oversight, and accuracy. Verify tools used with students are compliant or used under teacher supervision.
GDPR
Personal data of minors requires explicit parental consent or a school-level Data Processing Agreement. Data must not be transferred outside the EU without adequate safeguards. Prefer EU-based data storage.
Council of Europe AI guidelines
Recommends human oversight, transparency, and non-discrimination in AI educational tools. Teachers retain full pedagogical responsibility regardless of tool use.
School / national policy
Many EU member states and schools have issued their own AI guidance. Always check your national ministry of education website and your school's IT/data policy before use.
Important

If no approved tool list exists at your school, consult your school leadership or data protection officer before using any AI tool with students. When in doubt, use the tool yourself and share only your own outputs with students.

4
Pre-use checklist ▼
0 of 7 completed
  • I have checked my school's approved tool list or consulted my data protection officer
  • The tool meets the minimum age requirement for my students
  • I have verified GDPR / data privacy compliance — no student personal data is required
  • I have tested the tool myself and it is appropriate for my subject and level
  • All students can access the tool equally — free tier or school licence is available
  • I have a plan to maintain teacher oversight during student use
  • I can explain to students at a basic level what the tool does and does not do
